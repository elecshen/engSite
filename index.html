Modeling and Simulation

Basic Concepts and Classification of Models

Basic Concepts Classification of Models Model Development Stages

1. Basic Concepts of Modeling Theory

Model: An object or description of an object/system for substitution under certain conditions.

Modeling: The process of building, studying, and applying models to study objects.

A model must replicate the properties of the original object as fully as possible while being simpler and more convenient for study.

Adequacy: The degree of conformity of results obtained from the developed model to experimental data or test tasks.

Properties of a model include finitude, simplicity, approximateness, adequacy, and informativeness.

2. Classification of Models

2.1. Classification by Degree of Abstraction from Original

Models are divided into material (physical) and ideal:

  • Material Models: Full-scale, quasi full-scale, scale, and analog models.
  • Ideal Models: Intuitive and symbolic models.

Symbolic models include linguistic, visual, graphical, and mathematical models.

2.2. Classification by Degree of Stability

Stable Systems: Return to their original state after disturbances.

Unstable Systems: Disturbances intensify over time.

2.3. Classification by Relation to External Factors

Closed Models: Function independently of external variables.

Open Models: Connected to external variables.

2.4. Classification by Relation to Time

Models can be continuous or discrete, static or dynamic.

Static Models: Do not include the time parameter.

Dynamic Models: Reflect a system in time.

3. Model Development Stages

Stage 1: Requirement Analysis and Design

Involves stating modeling goals, collecting data, building a conceptual model, and verifying its authenticity.

Stage 2: Model Development

Selecting tools, developing a logical model, defining module properties, setting simulation time, and verifying the model.

Stage 3: Experimentation

Running simulations, varying parameters, collecting statistics, and analyzing results.

Stage 4: Finalizing Results

Evaluating work, comparing goals with results, and preparing a final report.
